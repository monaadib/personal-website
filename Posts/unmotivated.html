on being unmotivated

  • july 12, 2019
  • some thoughts
  • by mona

Cindy met up with her parents in London, so we parted ways and I did some exploring on my own for two of the three days. I loved my time with Cindy but it was cool to be able to do things in my own pace and have the time and space to do some thinking. During my time at university, I've realized that I'm the type of person who needs quite a lot of time alone during the day, so it was nice to have that alone time.


Growing up, I loved reading about famous European families -- the Habsburgs, the Medicis and of course the Tudors. Seeing the Tower of London and Hampton Court in person made my heart full. It felt like the imagination of my ten-year-old self came to life.


Some stuff that I loved in London:

  • At Bushy Park near Hampton Court, there's a bunch of deer chilling around.
  • The cardamom buns at Black Swan Yard Coffee.
  • After Henry VIII had Anne Boleyn killed, he had every sign of her removed at Hampton Court Palace. Well, all except one, which was missed. In the corner of the Grand Hall, there is a carving of the intertwined letters H and A, for Henry and Anne Boleyn.
  • After Jane Seymour died, apparently Henry kept a lock of her hair in his bible until he died.
  • Hearing Vicky's London stories.
  • Taking a nap at Hyde Park.
