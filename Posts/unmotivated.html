on being unmotivated

  • july 12, 2019
  • some thoughts
  • by mona

I am unmotivated.


This three word revelation is nothing new to me. I've been thinking about this unsettling feeling for a while. I've talked to others, read countless forums (and by forums i mean reddit and only reddit) and tried watching motivational videos on YouTube, but the feeling still persists. I've gotten a lot reassurance, often reminded that this is normal. This is temporary. I shouldn't worry. But of course, I don't feel normal and man, oh man, I've been worrying.


I've also gotten a lot of advice, the kind of stuff that I should have been doing anyway: exercising, reading, drinking water (and i guess the water in coffee doesn't count?!?), sleeping earlier, putting down my phone. Who knows? Maybe reddit is right. Maybe a cup of water a day keeps the unmotivated feelings away.


After all my thinking, I've come up with some theories about the origin of this feeling.


  • 1) I'm burnt out, for which perhaps the cure is time.
  • 2) I'm in this in-between stage as one chapter of my life recently ended and the next is soon starting. I guess my body is at a stand-still in anticipation of that next chapter.

And the one that's the most unsettling:


  • 3) I'm uninspired.

I have this intuitive feeling that while the first two may be partly contributing to my lack of motivation, the third theory is my main problem. I've been uninspired for a while, even before the summer break started, but I used my assignments, exams and extracurricular commitments to temporarily push those thoughts away. "I'll think about it later", I would say, always acknowledging that the thoughts were ever present, but that the time wasn't right to deal with them. I guess I don't have an excuse to push the thoughts away anymore.

I started 2019 really excited. I had all these ideas about what I wanted to do -- things that are really mundane to most, but felt different and meaningful to me. I wanted to get my driver's license, go clubbing for the first time, go to my first dance class and ask acquaintances I admired out on coffee dates. I did most of these things, and felt really good about them, but somewhere along in the following months, I stopped having new ideas. Instead of being excited about trying something new, I get excited about going home.


I'm not sure what to do. Maybe I'll start watching videos about "how to get inspired". Maybe I'll find myself during a hike. Maybe I'll force myself to read and watch movies and draw and dance, and the inspiration will hit me one day.


Or maybe I'll just go home -- that sounds good for now.
