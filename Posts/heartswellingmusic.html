Running List of Heart-Swelling Music

  • august 3, 2018
  • some lists
  • by mona

I know pretty quickly if a song is special to me. My skin gets all goosebumpy and my chest feels like it's going to explode. I feel a tug at my heart and my body feels warm and overwhelmed but in the best way possible. I don't know why they make me feel the way I do. I can't pinpoint what I specifically like about the songs. I just know that time after time, they make me feel a different, special way. Here is a running list (in no particular order) of the songs:


  • The Ink Spots and Ella Fitzgerald's "I'm Making Believe"
  • The Ink Spots' "If I Didn't Care"
  • The Beach Boys' "Don't Worry Baby"
  • The Platters' "Twilight Time"
  • The Cure's "Close to Me"
  • Tony Bennet and Lady Gaga's version of "Check to Check"
  • Michael Buble's version of "It's Beginning to Look A Lot Like Christmas"
  • The Jonas Brothers' "Lovebug"
  • Howard Shore's "Concerning Hobbits"
  • Bob Dylan and Johnny Cash's "Girl from the North Country"
  • Sam Cooke's "A Change is Gonna Come"
  • Dean Martin's "On An Evening in Roma"
  • The Beatles' "And I Love Her"
  • Arcade Fire's "The Suburbs"
  • Arctic Monkeys' "No Buses"

Here is a Spotify playlist of the songs (I'm very very hip and technologically advanced). Also, the Jonas Brothers' Lovebug isn't on Spotify but y'all should revisit that gem on YouTube. Additional important note: after writing the list, I'm very aware of the lack of female singers, writers and producers. Oh girl, there's so many icons I have to catch-up on.
