Brussels

  • july 18, 2018
  • some travels
  • by mona

I really vibed with Brussels. I think the hipster-wannabe inside of me was in awe of all of the coffee shops and minimalistic clothing stores.


For the sake of some good photos, I wish I was just a floating head. I asked Cindy to help me take my next profile picture. During the whole process, I was very aware of my discomfort. I didn't know what to do with my legs or where to put my hands. Whenever I see photos of my friends on social media, I always think: "Wow. This is beautiful! I should pose exactly like that!" But then in front of a camera, I become aware that I can't pose like them. I'm not sure if what I feel has to do with a lack of self-confidence. Sometimes I think the discomfort comes from the acknowledgement that the person I am and the person I'm trying to be in photos aren't the same [*note to self: I'm pretty sure there is a theory in psychology about the discomfort that comes with the disconnect between who you really are and who you think you are]. I want to look as perfect as my friends but in front of the camera, I feel dishonest.


Anyways, here are some of the photos. Shout-out to Cindy for the cool photos and for being patient with me.

*response to my own note: I went through my psychology notes and found this under the subsection about self-esteem: "when they become aware that actual self is inconsistent with self they want to be, they feel anxious". I just want to take a moment to question the quality of my notes, but yes I guess my discomfort is a thing taught in psychology courses?!?
