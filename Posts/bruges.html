Bruges

  • July 23, 2018
  • some travels
  • by mona

When I was in Bruges, I kept on thinking about how much I would have loved the city when I was twelve years old. It looks like it came straight out of a Middle Ages textbook or a fantasy novel. The city is filled with canals and cobbled streets, and every building feels like it has some significant history to it. As cliched as it sounds (side note: I feel like saying "as cliched as it sounds" is also very clich√© but 4 am is not a time for original thought so I'll just let it go), I really did feel like I had been transported to a different time. As I walked through the old part of the city, it didn't feel like the past and present had integrated into one city. I only felt the past. I think that was a good, humbling reminder that I am and that we are all an incredibly small part in an incredibly long timeline.


Some things that I liked in Bruges:


  • There are a lot of tea rooms
  • Audrey Hepburn as a cat on a pillowcase
  • Medieval sigils everywhere
  • The cup of hot chocolate that Cindy and I shared (and the second cup was good too)
  • Skyscraper [The Bruges Whale]: Whale made up of plastic garbage found in oceans around the world and one of those objects was a recycling bin. Put my own impact into perspective.
  • Walking through a monastery where you had to speak quietly and couldn't step on the grass?!?
