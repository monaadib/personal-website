Antwerp

  • july 25, 2018
  • some travels
  • by mona

My favourite things during my time at Antwerp were grabbing a coffee, sandwich and fruit at the market near the Stadsschouwburg (which was next to our Airbnb) and visiting the Plantin-Moretus Museum which is the only surviving printing workshop dating back to the Renaissance. It was a humbling experience to be surrounded by the work of a family who did so much in the sharing of knowledge.


Some wonderful things I learned in Antwerp that made me laugh:

  • During the early 1600s, the "Windmill Cup" was poppin' at parties. It's a cup with a mini windmill at the bottom, so you cannot put the cup down unless it's empty. To start the game, players set the windmill's sails in motion. As long as the sails are moving, the player is allowed to drink. If the cup isn't empty when the sails stop moving, the hour-hand on the windmill's clock indicates how many extra cups the loser has to drink.
  • In 1605, the city council of Antwerp bought Adrien Bouchier's medicine against the plague; Bouchier gave the council bottles filled with water.

Here is a photo of me sitting in the hand of Antwerp. According to a legend, the city's name comes from a story about the giant Antigoon who collected a toll from boatmen travelling on the Scheldt river. For anyone who refused to pay, the giant cut off one of their hands. Then comes the young hero Silivius Brabo who manages to kill the giant. He cuts off giant's hand and throws it into the river. The legend says that the name 'Antwerpen' comes from the Dutch "hand werpen" meaning "hand thrown". Most likely the name actually comes from "anda" (at) and "werpum" (wharf). The Brabo Fountain in front of Antwerp's City Hall shows the story.
