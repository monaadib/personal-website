A blog? A blog!

  • July 9, 2018
  • some thoughts
  • by mona

I've tried writing a journal before. The first few days were great. I would eagerly write out my thoughts and experiences thinking: "Oh man, how cool will it be to look back on these words in ten years". After a few days, I stopped, overwhelmed by the silliness of seeing my own daily thoughts on paper.


It still feels a bit silly. But this time, it also feels necessary.


I started that journal because I wanted to have something to look back on. A way to remember the little moments -- those conversations or self-reflections that felt incredibly important and made me feel something different, something special. I wanted a book of memories that would make some adult version of Mona smile and reminisce on her youth.


I still want that. But I also want more. Over the past few years, I've realized that I'm not confident. I don't feel like myself when I'm around new people. Even around people I consider friends, I change myself, thinking that that's what I need to do so that they will like me. It's hard for me to share the kinds of things I like and to be honest about how I feel.


It feels silly to write that I'm not confident, but I think that's what I need. With this blog, I'm hoping to challenge myself to get out of my comfort-zone which means doing things that I wouldn't usually do. Anxious about being in front of a camera? I'll try vlogging! Scared to meet new people? I'll introduce myself anyway! Find all of this a bit silly? Accept that it is, and that one day all that internal screaming will (probably) stop.


To be honest, this much optimism doesn't even sound like me. So, just as a reminder to myself, @Mona, it's okay to feel uncomfortable and to not want to do things that others want to do. Just be your honest-self which means that sometimes you'll have to say: "No, I don't like that. That's not me."


With all of that said, welcome to the blog.
